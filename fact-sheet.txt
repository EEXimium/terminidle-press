ONE-LINE PITCH
--------------
An incremental hacking game played inside a living terminal: start with a
blinking cursor and one command, end up running an entire operating system
— then wipe it all for quantum power and do it again, stronger.

ELEVATOR PITCH
--------------
TERMINIDLE starts you with nothing. Not "few resources" nothing — actual
nothing: no mouse cursor (that's an upgrade), no sound (speakers are
hardware you buy), no windows (comfort is earned). Type 'mine', watch one
byte arrive, and build from there: botnets, a skill tree, typed hacking
contracts, a crypto exchange, an overclockable rig that can literally burn
out. Push far enough and the game outgrows the terminal itself — a full
desktop OS boots around it, with arcade games that pay real data, a darknet
chat full of suspiciously human bots, and a map of the internet to conquer.
Ascend, lose everything but the quantum layer, and climb back faster.
